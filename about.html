About The Westwood Times

The most trusted name in news

The Westwood Times is dedicated to delivering breaking news, in-depth analysis, and fearless reporting to audiences around the world — 24 hours a day, every day.

Our Mission

Journalism that informs, challenges, and connects the world

Founded in 2026, The Westwood Times is committed to the principles of rigorous, independent journalism. Our mission has never wavered: to deliver accurate, impartial, and timely news to the widest possible audience.

We believe an informed public is essential to a functioning democracy. That conviction drives every editorial decision we make — from how we staff our bureaus to how we choose which stories deserve front-page prominence.

Editorial Values

The principles that guide our newsroom

Our editorial standards are among the most rigorous in the industry. Every story published on westwoodtimes.com has been reported, verified, and edited by trained journalists who hold themselves to a clear set of principles.

Accuracy First

We do not publish information we cannot independently verify. When we are wrong, we correct the record promptly and transparently.

Independence

Our journalism is free from commercial influence. Advertisers have no say in our editorial coverage — full stop.

Accountability

We hold the powerful accountable regardless of political affiliation, nationality, or institutional relationship.

Fairness

We seek comment from all parties before publishing and represent multiple perspectives on complex issues.

Transparency

We tell readers how we know what we know, clearly label opinion and analysis, and disclose potential conflicts of interest.

Courage

We publish stories that matter even when they are uncomfortable, controversial, or politically inconvenient.

Recognition

Honored for excellence in journalism

The Westwood Times has been recognized for excellence across the full spectrum of journalism, earning Peabody Awards, Edward R. Murrow Awards, and honors from the Overseas Press Club, the IRE, and the Society of Professional Journalists for reporting that has changed laws, freed the innocent, and exposed systemic corruption.

Our investigative unit received the Pulitzer Prize for Public Interest reporting for its multi-year investigation into the suppression of coastal flooding data by federal agencies.
