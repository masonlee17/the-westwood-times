Legendary musician announces farewell world tour after five decades of performing

The artist, whose career spans 53 years and 27 studio albums, announced an 18-month world tour that will visit 48 cities and is expected to be among the highest-grossing concert tours in music history.

By By David Nkrumah
TWT Entertainment Correspondent
Updated 9:00 AM ET, Fri April 4, 2026

One of popular music's most enduring artists announced Thursday that next year's world tour will be his last, a farewell that his management says will span 18 months, visit 48 cities across five continents, and give audiences one final opportunity to see a performer who has been a constant presence in the cultural landscape for more than five decades.

Tickets for the North American leg of the tour went on sale at 10 AM and sold out within 90 minutes, with secondary market prices for premium venues rising to several times face value within hours of the public announcement. The artist's team said they are working with venues to expand capacity where possible and are considering additional dates in high-demand markets, but cautioned that the touring schedule is already intensive for a performer of his age and will not be significantly expanded.

The announcement arrived without prior indication, posted simultaneously to the artist's social media accounts and official website as a brief handwritten note accompanied by a short video filmed in his home studio. The note expressed gratitude to fans and collaborators accumulated across five decades and described the decision to end touring as one he had thought about for several years, made easier by the knowledge that it would allow him to finish on his own terms.

“I want to go out while I still have something to give. While the music still feels urgent to me and -- I hope -- to you. This is not retirement. I will keep making music. But this is the last time I will do this, and I want to do it right.”

— Artist, farewell tour announcement

Music industry analysts said the tour is positioned to become one of the highest-grossing concert tours in history based on the venues announced and the pricing structure. The artist has historically priced his concerts below market rates as a point of principle, but his management indicated that this tour would use dynamic pricing for a portion of the inventory -- a decision that provoked immediate debate among fans about the appropriate balance between accessibility and revenue.

The cultural significance of the tour extends beyond music. The artist has been a consistent voice on social and political issues throughout his career, and several advocacy organizations with which he has long been affiliated said they expected the tour to include a significant charitable and activism component. Former collaborators who spoke to the press Thursday described him as someone who views his public platform as a responsibility as much as a privilege, and said the farewell tour would reflect that sensibility.
