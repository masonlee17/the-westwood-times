Startup valued at $12 billion secures new round of funding to expand clean energy grid storage

Investors poured $1.4 billion into the clean energy storage company in a funding round that valued the startup at $12 billion, betting that its novel battery chemistry and grid integration software can deliver on promises to cut energy storage costs by up to 60%.

By By Daniel Reeves
TWT Business Correspondent
Updated 9:00 AM ET, Fri April 4, 2026

A clean energy storage startup closed a $1.4 billion funding round Thursday, reaching a $12 billion valuation and cementing its status as one of the most heavily capitalized companies in the rapidly expanding grid-scale battery sector. The capital will be used to scale manufacturing of the company's proprietary battery chemistry and expand deployment of its grid integration software across new markets.

The funding round was led by a consortium of infrastructure-focused investment funds and a strategic participation from two major utilities, a combination that investors said signals the technology is approaching commercial maturity rather than remaining in the demonstration phase. The company has deployed its storage systems at 14 utility-scale sites over the past three years, accumulating operational data that independent evaluators say supports its claims of cost and performance advantages over conventional lithium-ion battery systems.

The company's chief executive told investors Thursday that the new capital would fund construction of a second domestic manufacturing facility and accelerate international expansion into markets where grid modernization is accelerating. She also outlined plans to expand the company's software platform, which manages the dispatch of stored energy in response to real-time grid conditions, into a standalone product that could be deployed alongside other storage technologies.

“The grid storage market is growing faster than anyone in this industry predicted three years ago. The question is no longer whether storage is commercially viable -- it clearly is. The question is who builds the dominant platform.”

— Company Chief Executive, investor briefing

The technology's core innovation is a battery chemistry that uses more abundant and less geopolitically concentrated materials than standard lithium-ion formulations, which rely heavily on cobalt and nickel supply chains concentrated in a small number of countries. The company's cells use iron and organic compounds instead, a substitution that reduces material costs and supply chain risk while accepting some trade-offs in energy density that are acceptable for stationary grid storage applications where weight is not a constraint.

Analysts covering the clean energy storage sector said the funding round reflects a broader shift in investor appetite toward infrastructure-grade, capital-intensive businesses that can generate stable, long-term cash flows from contracted utility agreements. The sector has attracted significant capital in recent months as interest rates have begun to decline, making the long-duration, infrastructure-like returns of energy storage projects more attractive relative to other asset classes.
